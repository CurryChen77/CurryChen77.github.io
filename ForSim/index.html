ForSim: Forward Simulation for Realistic Interactive Traffic Scenarios

1School of Vehicle and Mobility, Tsinghua University

ForSim is a forward-simulation framework designed to synthesize realistic, interactive traffic scenarios for closed-loop AV evaluation. It decouples scene rollout from trajectory alignment, enabling controllable yet natural interactions across diverse map topologies.

Abstract

Closed-loop traffic simulation requires realistic interactions while preserving controllability for evaluation. ForSim introduces a forward rollout that proposes candidate trajectories, followed by a trajectory-alignment stage that reconciles inter-agent consistency. This two-stage formulation enables scalable scenario generation, stable interaction dynamics, and faithful reproduction of long-horizon behaviors for AV benchmarking.

Method Overview

Basic Rollout

Predict multi-agent rollouts with open-loop dynamics as an initial proposal set.

Step Rollout

Refine agent states step-by-step to capture short-horizon interaction cues.

Trajectory Alignment

Align candidate trajectories to enforce multi-agent consistency and realism.
